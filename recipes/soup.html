Chinese Rice Soup

Description

I made this recipe twice, and it was super delicious. You can't buy cod in Dutch supermarkets, so I bought pangasius fillet. Next time, however, I would prefer to buy a different kind of white fish, because I found out that pangasius fillet is fished in the Mekong Delta in Vietnam, which is one of the most polluted rivers in the world.

Ingredients

  • 1000 ml chicken stock, low sodium
  • 250 ml water
  • 2 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1.5 cm ginger piece, sliced
  • 1.5 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1.5 tbsp Chinese cooking wine
  • 0.25 - 0.5 tsp sesame oil, toasted (optional)
  • 0.25 tsp white pepper
  • 150 g uncooked white rice
  • 3 stems choy sum, cut into 2 cm lengths
  • 250 g white fish fillets, cut into 2.5 cm cubes
  • 1 green onion, finely sliced
  • Crispy fried shallots (optional)
  • Crunchy fried noodles (optional)
  • Chili oil or paste (optional)

Steps

  1. Combine chicken stock, water, soy sauce, Chinese cooking wine, garlic, and ginger in a small pot. Bring to a simmer over high heat, then reduce to medium and simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Add the rice and cook uncovered for 10 minutes until the broth is gently bubbling.
  3. Add the fish and choy sum stems to the broth and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  4. Remove the garlic and ginger pieces from the soup. Stir in the choy sum leaves, sesame oil, and white pepper. Serve immediately with desired toppings.
